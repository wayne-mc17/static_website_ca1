Welcome


Drogheda is located at the mouth of the River Boyne, the towns name comes from the Irish ‘Droichead Átha’, meaning bridge of the ford. Drogheda was originally two separate towns on either side of the river in the late 12th century. The south side in Meath was founded by Hugh De Lacy. The northern side in Louth was established by Bertram De Verdon. They had separate corporations, taxes, tariffs and landing charges for the port. This led to a fierce rivalry and a lot of blood was spilled over the years, with the two sides of the town battling for foreign trade.
